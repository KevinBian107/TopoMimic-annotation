# Auto-labeling rules

The annotation GUI has two independent automated pipelines that read a
per-clip qpos CSV (track or stac) and produce timeline annotations:

1. **Auto-label** — assigns one of 5 macro behaviors to every frame.
2. **Auto-directionality** — assigns one of `{Left, Right, Straight}` to
   every frame, on a separate track rendered above the behavior rows.

Both pipelines share the same qpos reader and feature helpers. Thresholds
live in `auto_label_config.json` and are re-generated by
`scripts/calibrate_thresholds.py` from the labeled clips.

---

## qpos column layout (inferred)

The qpos CSV is 74 columns wide (`q0 … q73`). Rules use:

| Columns | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `q0, q1` | Root planar position (x, y) |
| `q2` | Root height (z) — "how high off the ground" |
| `q3, q4, q5, q6` | Root orientation quaternion (w, x, y, z) |
| `q7 … q40` | Forelimb + upper-body joints (forelimb-activity proxy) |
| `q41 … q73` | Hindlimb + tail joints (hindlimb-activity proxy) |

If a future rig re-orders the qpos layout, these column ranges need to be
revalidated or the thresholds will misfire.

---

## Per-frame features

Computed in a sliding 15-frame window (~0.5 s at 30 fps):

| Feature | Definition |
|---|---|
| `v_xy` | Planar velocity: RMS of `diff(q0)`, `diff(q1)` combined. "How much the body is translating on the floor." |
| `v_z` | Vertical velocity: RMS of `diff(q2)`. "How fast the body is rising or falling." |
| `z_abs` | Raw `q2[i]`. Root height in absolute terms. |
| `z_rel` | `q2[i] − median(q2 over clip)`. Height relative to this clip's baseline. |
| `yaw_rate` | RMS of `diff(unwrap(yaw))` where yaw is derived from `q3..q6`. "How fast the body is rotating about the vertical axis." |
| `forelimb_var` | Mean variance across `q7..q40` in the window. "How much the forelimbs / upper body are moving." |
| `hindlimb_var` | Mean variance across `q41..q73` in the window. "How much the hindlimbs are moving." |
| `yaw` | The unwrapped yaw angle itself (signed). Used by auto-directionality. |

## Macro-behavior rules

Evaluated **first match wins, per frame**. Priority reflects the
physical ordering the user specified: posture wins over motion, stationary
rotation wins over translation, grooming and walking are disambiguated by
whether the body is moving planar.

```
priority: Rear > Turn > Groom > Walk > Immobile
```

### 1. Rear — "the animal is up on its hind legs"

**What we look for**: root is high, OR root is rising. The animal does not
have to be translating or rotating — a still rear counts.

**Features**: `z_abs`, `z_rel`

**Conditions**:
- `z_abs > rear_z_abs`, OR
- `z_rel > rear_z_delta`

**Why this excludes Turn and Walk**: Rear is pure posture. A rotating-while-
rearing animal is still classified Rear because the user's gloss was
*"Rear has high root z, so not turning"*. A walking-while-rearing gait
does not physically occur in this dataset.

**Known failure modes**:
- Absolute threshold is rig-dependent. A very small rig where the animal's
  nominal standing z is already close to `rear_z_abs` will produce
  false-positive Rears.
- Brief lift-offs that barely clear the threshold become 1–2 frame Rears
  and are absorbed by the flicker filter.

### 2. Turn — "the animal rotates in place"

**What we look for**: body rotates about the vertical axis AND the body is
not translating AND the body is not up on hind legs. Can be on 2 or 4 limbs.

**Features**: `v_xy`, `yaw_rate`

**Conditions** (rule 1 did not fire):
- `v_xy < walk_xy`, AND
- `|yaw_rate| > orient_yaw`

**Why this excludes Walk**: the user's gloss was *"Turn must be stationary.
Walk + Turn would not happen."* Explicitly gated by `v_xy < walk_xy`.

**Known failure modes**:
- A walking animal that curves sharply produces both high `v_xy` and high
  `yaw_rate`. By priority, this is Walk, not Turn.
- Slow rotational drift below `orient_yaw` falls through to Immobile.

### 3. Groom — "sitting on hind legs, forepaws busy"

**What we look for**: forelimbs active AND hindlimbs planted AND body not
translating AND body not elevated (rule 1 would have fired).

**Features**: `v_xy`, `forelimb_var`, `hindlimb_var`

**Conditions** (rules 1–2 did not fire):
- `v_xy < walk_xy`, AND
- `forelimb_var > groom_limb`, AND
- `hindlimb_var < still_hindlimb`

**Why this excludes Immobile**: Immobile has both fore- and hind-limb
variance low. Groom's signature is the *asymmetry*: hindlimbs still,
forelimbs working.

**Known failure modes**:
- If the animal grooms while occasionally repositioning hindlimbs, the
  `hindlimb_var` guard fails and frames fall through to Walk or Immobile.
  This is a conservative miss — the user can promote from the context menu.
- On this dataset, manually-labeled Grooming frames sometimes sit in
  near-Immobile stretches because manual labels may include paused-paw
  moments. Those frames will auto-label as Immobile.

### 4. Walk — "translating on the floor"

**What we look for**: body is translating in xy.

**Features**: `v_xy`

**Conditions** (rules 1–3 did not fire):
- `v_xy > walk_xy`

**Why this excludes Turn**: Turn requires `v_xy < walk_xy`. If the animal
is moving AND rotating, it lands in Walk because the stationary guard in
Turn fails first.

**Known failure modes**:
- Passive slides (the animal shifts without a gait signature) are labeled
  Walk. If you want stricter walking detection, add a four-limb-active
  guard (both fore- and hind-limb variance above a threshold).

### 5. Immobile — "default: nothing else fired"

**What we look for**: no posture or motion signal was detected. All 4
limbs on the ground, not moving much.

**Features**: implicit — no rule-1-to-4 match.

**Conditions**: none of the above.

**Known failure modes**:
- Rule interpretation is fall-through, not positive detection. A frame
  with garbled qpos (NaNs, zeros) will land here.

## Post-grouping smoothing

After per-frame labels are computed:

1. **Flicker filter** (`min_segment_duration = 0.25 s`): any segment shorter
   than 0.25 s is merged into the previous segment. Protects against
   single-window jitters.
2. **Bridging** (`bridge_threshold = 0.5 s`): if three consecutive segments
   form a pattern `A → B → A` where `duration(B) < 0.5 s`, they are
   collapsed into one `A`. Smooths out brief interruptions — a Turn that
   includes a very short Immobile becomes one continuous Turn.

Bridging runs once. It is not idempotent in theory, but in practice one
pass is sufficient because the merges rarely create new A-B-A triples.

## Auto-directionality (separate pipeline)

### What we look for

Continuous track of `{Left, Right, Straight}` across every frame, smoothed
to avoid flicker and hold for long stretches.

### Features

- `yaw` (signed, unwrapped) — computed from `q3..q6` exactly as in the
  auto-labeler.
- `signed_yaw_rate` — centered difference of `yaw` over a 15-frame window,
  **signed** (not RMS). Positive = rotating counterclockwise (→ Left),
  negative = clockwise (→ Right).

### Per-frame rule

- `signed_yaw_rate > +dir_yaw_thresh` → **Left**
- `signed_yaw_rate < −dir_yaw_thresh` → **Right**
- otherwise → **Straight**

### Smoothing

After per-frame labels, contiguous same-label runs are grouped, then any
segment shorter than `dir_min_duration` (default 1.0 s) is merged into
whichever neighbor has the larger summed `|signed_yaw_rate|`. This means
brief direction flips fold into the more "decisive" surrounding segment,
producing a non-flickering track.

### Known failure modes

- If the animal is moving its head independently of its body, yaw derived
  from root orientation may disagree with what a human sees. We use root
  quaternion (q3..q6), not head quaternion.
- The min-duration smoothing can cause a genuine short turn to get merged
  into a longer surrounding Straight. Raise `dir_min_duration` to
  preserve more short turns; lower it to reduce lag.

## Calibration

Run `python scripts/calibrate_thresholds.py <labels.csv> <qpos_dir> <out.json>`
to regenerate `auto_label_config.json`. The script:

1. Reads the labeled CSV and groups frames by behavior.
2. Computes each feature's distribution per class (median, p10, p90).
3. Picks thresholds where the target class's distribution is cleanly
   separated from the rest. Falls back to midpoints when strict
   separation fails, with explicit printed reasoning.
4. Adds `dir_yaw_thresh` from the legacy Left/Right Turn rows: instantaneous
   signed yaw-rate inside those spans vs outside.

The printed per-class distributions are a critical sanity check. If a
feature you expect to separate classes shows overlapping medians, the
heuristic will be weak and the associated class will be under- or
over-detected.

## Invariants (for developers)

- **Immobile segments have no direction** in the old flag model. In the
  new two-track model, direction lives on its own track and Immobile
  segments are unaffected.
- **Turn requires `v_xy < walk_xy`**. The priority is encoded in the
  ordering of the if-else chain in `_classify_frame`; do not rearrange
  without updating this document.
- **Bridging does not traverse the direction track**. The two tracks are
  independent.
